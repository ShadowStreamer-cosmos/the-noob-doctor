What are the Sokolow-Lyon Criteria?

Definition: It is the most widely recognized electrocardiographic (ECG) rule used to diagnose Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH). As the left ventricular muscle thickens, it generates larger electrical vectors during depolarization, which translates to increased voltages on the precordial ECG leads.

The Primary Rule: The criteria are met if the amplitude of the S wave in lead V1 + the R wave in lead V5 or V6 is ≥ 35 mm (where 1 mm = 0.1 mV on a standard ECG grid).

Historical Origin

The criteria were developed by cardiologists Dr. Maurice Sokolow and Dr. Thomas P. Lyon in 1949. They published a landmark paper in the American Heart Journal analyzing the electrocardiograms of 421 patients with known causes of LVH (such as severe hypertension and aortic valve disease). By systematically measuring the waveforms and comparing them against clinical findings, they established these specific millimeter cutoffs. This provided physicians with a rapid, non-invasive, and standardized bedside tool to screen for LVH decades before echocardiography became widely available.
