Genetic Basis of Autoimmunity

Core Concept in One Line

Autoimmunity = Loss of self-tolerance → immune system attacks its own tissues, driven by genetics + environment.

HLA Associations — High Yield
HLA-DR4 → Rheumatoid Arthritis RA-specific
HLA-B27 → Spondyloarthropathies (AS 90%) SpA
HLA-DR3 → SLE, Sjögren's, DM1
Mnemonic
"B27 = Backbone"
B27 → Back (spine) problems = Spondyloarthritis
DR4 = "Doctor of the 4 knuckles" = RA (MCP joints)
PTPN22 — The Master Switch
Normally: PTPN22 = brake on T-cell activation
SNP R620W → brake fails → T cells hyperactive
Links: RA, SLE, DM1, Graves', Vitiligo, MG, Addison's
EXAM TRAP: HLA-DR4 = RA-specific. PTPN22 = shared across many autoimmunes. Don't confuse them.

Rheumatoid Arthritis

02
Pathophysiology — Step by Step
  • 1
    Genetic hit (HLA-DR4) + trigger (smoking, infection) → immune activation
  • 2
    Confused plasma cells produce RF (attacks IgG) + Anti-CCP (attacks citrullinated proteins)
  • 3
    CD4+ T cells infiltrate synovium → macrophages activated
  • 4
    Cytokine storm: TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6, IL-17 released
  • 5
    Synovial hyperplasia → Pannus formation (invasive granulation tissue)
  • 6
    Pannus erodes cartilage and bone → irreversible joint destruction
Mnemonic — Cytokines in RA
"TNF-α Is Like 1 (IL-1) Liter (IL-17) of 6 (IL-6) trouble"
TNF-α → IL-1 → IL-6 → IL-17 = the four culprits in RA synovitis
Classic Clinical Features
Morning stiffness >30 min — improves with activity
Symmetric polyarthritis — MCP and PIP joints
Spares DIP joints (key differentiator from OA)
Extra-articular: Nodules, ILD, vasculitis, anemia
DIP rule: RA spares DIP. OA loves DIP (Heberden's nodes). Psoriatic arthritis attacks DIP.
Deformities — Visual Memory
Boutonnière Deformity
MCP: neutral → PIP: [FLEXED ↓] → DIP: [EXTENDED ↑]
Central slip rupture. Like a button poking through a hole.
Swan Neck Deformity
MCP: neutral → PIP: [EXTENDED ↑] → DIP: [FLEXED ↓]
Mallet injury / tight intrinsics. Looks like a swan's neck curve.
Ulnar Deviation
All fingers drift toward the pinky side. Classic RA at MCP joints.
Mnemonic
Boutonnière = Button + hole = PIP collapses IN
Swan neck = S-curve = PIP goes UP like swan head
Diagnosis — 2010 ACR/EULAR Criteria (Score ≥ 6 = RA)
DomainWhat to ScorePoints

Management Pyramid — Step-Up Strategy
Step 1 — Symptom Control: NSAIDs + short-course steroids (bridge therapy)
Step 2 — csDMARDs: Methotrexate (anchor drug, always first) ± Hydroxychloroquine ± Sulfasalazine ± Leflunomide
Step 3 — bDMARDs: TNF-i (Etanercept, Adalimumab, Infliximab) | IL-6-i (Tocilizumab) | Abatacept | Rituximab
Step 4 — tsDMARDs: JAK inhibitors (Tofacitinib, Upadacitinib)
NEVER forget: Methotrexate = anchor. All combinations are built around MTX. Only switch if patient cannot tolerate MTX.

Primary Raynaud's

  • • Standalone, no CTD
  • • Mild, no tissue damage
  • • Young women
  • • Normal nailfold capillaries

Secondary Raynaud's (SSc)

  • • With connective tissue disease
  • • Digital ulcers, gangrene possible
  • • Abnormal nailfold capillaries
  • • Anti-centromere or Scl-70 positive

Seronegative Spondyloarthritis

04
The SpA Group — Remember All 5
Mnemonic — PAREA
P-A-R-E-U
Psoriatic Arthritis
Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS)
Reactive Arthritis (formerly Reiter's)
Enteropathic Arthritis (IBD-associated)
Undifferentiated SpA

All are: RF-negative (seronegative) + HLA-B27 positive (varying degrees)
Shared Features Across All SpA
Inflammatory back pain — insidious onset, <40yr, improves with exercise (KEY differentiator from mechanical pain)
Enthesitis — inflammation at tendon/ligament insertion (Achilles, plantar fascia)
Asymmetric peripheral arthritis — lower limbs predominantly
Dactylitis — "sausage digit" (whole finger/toe swollen)
Extra-articular (3 Es)
Eyes: Acute anterior Uveitis (emergency — pain + red eye)
Enteritis: IBD association
rEactive: Psoriasis (skin)
B27 association: AS=90%, Reactive=70%, PsA=50%, IBD-SpA=25%
Ankylosing Spondylitis — High Yield
Clinical Features
Sacroiliitis → bamboo spine on X-ray
Reduced chest expansion (measure with tape)
Alternating buttock pain (characteristic)
Schober's test: <5cm increase = positive
Uveitis (acute anterior) — urgent ophthalmology
Cardiac: Aortic regurgitation (late)
Management
  • 1
    NSAIDs — first-line (Naproxen, Indomethacin). Also diagnostic — if pain resolves → AS!
  • 2
    Exercise — cornerstone. Never rest! Activity reduces stiffness.
  • 3
    Sulfasalazine — for peripheral arthritis only (not axial)
  • 4
    TNF inhibitors — if inadequate NSAID response
  • 5
    IL-17i (Secukinumab) — second biologic choice
KEY: MTX does NOT work for axial SpA. Sulfasalazine only for peripheral joints. Biologics = definitive for refractory AS.
Mnemonic — Reactive Arthritis (formerly Reiter's)
"Can't See, Can't Pee, Can't Climb a Tree"
See: Conjunctivitis/Uveitis  |  Pee: Urethritis  |  Tree: Arthritis (can't walk/climb)
Trigger: post-Chlamydia (urogenital) or post-Salmonella/Shigella (enteric)

"CANT Excrete"

Gout vs Pseudogout — The Crystal Comparison (Most Tested)
GOUT — MSU Crystals
Shape: Needle-shaped (thin, sharp, pointy)
Birefringence: Negatively birefringent
Color under polarized light: Yellow (parallel to compensator)
Joint: 1st MTP (Big toe) — Podagra
Age: 30–50, male predominance
X-ray: punched-out erosions, overhanging edge
PSEUDOGOUT — CPPD
Shape: Rhomboid (blunt, rectangular ends)
Birefringence: Weakly positive
Color: Blue (parallel to compensator)
Joint: Knee (most common)
Age: >60, elderly
X-ray: Chondrocalcinosis (calcification of cartilage)
Ultimate Crystal Mnemonic
GOUT = "Needles that are Negative & Yellow"
PSEUDO = "Rhomboid, positive, Blue — like a psuedo-diamond"

Memory hook: Real gout = real pain = sharp NEEDLES. Pseudo = fake diamond shape (rhomboid) in old knees.
Acute Gout Management
  • 1
    NSAIDs — Indomethacin, Naproxen. First-line if no contraindications
  • 2
    Colchicine — 1.2mg then 0.6mg 1hr later. Works best if started within 12–24hrs
  • 3
    Steroids — oral/IA. Use if NSAIDs and colchicine contraindicated (renal failure)
  • 4
    Anakinra (IL-1 blocker) — refractory gout
Do NOT start allopurinol during acute attack — it prolongs the flare.
Chronic Gout — Urate Lowering Therapy
  • 1
    Allopurinol — xanthine oxidase inhibitor. First-line. Target uric acid <6 mg/dL
  • 2
    Febuxostat — more potent XO inhibitor. Use if allopurinol intolerant
  • 3
    Probenecid — uricosuric. Only if underexcretor + good renal function
  • 4
    Pegloticase — IV, for refractory tophaceous gout
Always start colchicine prophylaxis 0.6mg daily for 3–6 months when initiating allopurinol to prevent flare.
